# Glossary of Email Marketing Terms

Understanding email marketing terminology is essential for success. This comprehensive glossary covers the most important terms you'll encounter in your email marketing journey.

## A

**A/B Testing (Split Testing)**  
A method of comparing two versions of an email to determine which performs better. Typically tests subject lines, content, CTAs, or send times with a portion of your list before sending the winning version to the remainder.

**Affirmative Consent**  
Explicit agreement by subscribers to receive marketing emails, typically through positive action like checking a box or clicking a button. Required by GDPR and many other privacy laws.

**Abandoned Cart Email**  
An automated email sent to customers who added items to their online shopping cart but didn't complete the purchase. Usually includes the cart contents and an incentive to complete the transaction.

**Above the Fold**  
The portion of an email visible without scrolling. Critical information and primary CTAs should appear here, as many recipients don't scroll.

**Acceptance Rate**  
The percentage of emails accepted by the receiving server. Calculated as: (Delivered emails ÷ Total sent) × 100.

**Alt Text (Alternative Text)**  
Descriptive text that appears when images don't load or for screen readers. Essential for accessibility and when images are blocked.

**API (Application Programming Interface)**  
A set of protocols that allows different software applications to communicate. Email service providers offer APIs for automation and integration.

**Authentication**  
The process of verifying that an email is legitimately from the sender it claims to be from. Includes SPF, DKIM, and DMARC protocols.

**Automation (Email Automation)**  
Emails sent automatically based on triggers, schedules, or subscriber actions. Examples include welcome series, birthday emails, and abandoned cart reminders.

## B

**Blacklist (Blocklist)**  
A list of IP addresses or domains identified as sources of spam. Being blacklisted severely impacts deliverability.

**Bounce**  
When an email cannot be delivered to the recipient's inbox. See Hard Bounce and Soft Bounce.

**Bounce Rate**  
The percentage of emails that couldn't be delivered. Calculated as: (Bounced emails ÷ Total sent) × 100.

**Bulk Email**  
Large-volume email sent to multiple recipients simultaneously. Must comply with anti-spam laws and best practices.

## C

**Call-to-Action (CTA)**  
A button, link, or instruction that encourages recipients to take a specific action, such as "Shop Now" or "Download Guide."

**CAN-SPAM Act**  
U.S. law establishing rules for commercial email, including requirements for unsubscribe options, accurate headers, and physical addresses.

**CASL (Canadian Anti-Spam Legislation)**  
Canadian law requiring explicit consent before sending commercial electronic messages, with strict penalties for violations.

**Cold Email**  
Unsolicited emails sent to prospects who have no prior relationship with the sender. Legal requirements vary by jurisdiction, with many requiring explicit consent or legitimate business interest.

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)**  
The percentage of email recipients who clicked one or more links. Calculated as: (Unique clicks ÷ Delivered emails) × 100.

**Click-to-Open Rate (CTOR)**  
The percentage of email openers who clicked a link. Calculated as: (Unique clicks ÷ Unique opens) × 100. Measures email content effectiveness.

**Commercial Email**  
Any email that promotes a commercial product or service, including promotional content and advertisements.

**Consent (Email Consent)**  
Explicit permission given by recipients to receive marketing emails. Must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. Required by GDPR, CASL, and other privacy laws.

**Complaint Rate**  
The percentage of recipients who mark your email as spam. Should stay below 0.1% to maintain good sender reputation.

**Conversion Rate**  
The percentage of recipients who completed a desired action (purchase, sign-up, download). Calculated as: (Conversions ÷ Delivered emails) × 100.

**CPM (Cost Per Mille/Thousand)**  
The cost per thousand emails sent. Common pricing model for email service providers.

## D

**Dedicated IP**  
An IP address used exclusively by one sender. Provides more control over sender reputation but requires consistent volume.

**Deliverability**  
The ability to successfully deliver emails to recipients' inboxes rather than spam folders or being blocked entirely.

**DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)**  
An authentication method that adds a digital signature to emails, verifying the message hasn't been altered in transit.

**DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance)**  
An email authentication protocol that builds on SPF and DKIM, telling receiving servers how to handle unauthenticated emails.

**Domain Reputation**  
The sending reputation associated with your domain name, influenced by engagement rates, complaints, and authentication.

**Double Opt-In (Confirmed Opt-In)**  
A subscription process requiring subscribers to confirm their email address via a confirmation email. Ensures higher list quality.

**Distribution List**  
A curated list of email addresses used to send messages to multiple recipients simultaneously. Also known as a mailing list.

**Drip Campaign**  
A series of pre-written emails sent automatically over time, often used for nurturing leads or onboarding new customers.

**Dynamic Content**  
Email content that changes based on subscriber data, preferences, or behavior, enabling personalization at scale.

## E

**Email Client**  
Software or web application used to read emails (Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, etc.). Each renders HTML differently.

**Email Service Provider (ESP)**  
A company that provides email marketing services, including sending infrastructure, templates, analytics, and list management.

**Engagement Rate**  
A composite metric measuring how recipients interact with your emails through opens, clicks, forwards, and replies.

## F

**Feedback Loop (FBL)**  
A service where ISPs notify senders when recipients mark their emails as spam, allowing removal of complainers.

**Forward Rate**  
The percentage of recipients who forwarded your email to others. Indicates content value and viral potential.

**From Line**  
The sender name and email address displayed to recipients. Should be recognizable and consistent.

## G

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**  
European Union regulation governing data protection and privacy, requiring explicit consent for email marketing to EU residents.

**Gray Mail (Graymail)**  
Email that recipients signed up for but no longer want. Not spam, but often treated similarly by recipients.

## H

**Hard Bounce**  
A permanent delivery failure due to invalid email addresses or non-existent domains. Should be removed immediately.

**Header**  
The top section of an email containing sender information, subject line, and technical routing data.

**Honey Pot (Honeypot)**  
A spam trap email address used to identify spammers. Never opted in and shouldn't be on legitimate lists.

**HTML Email**  
Email formatted with HTML code, allowing for design elements, images, and formatting beyond plain text.

## I

**Inbox Placement Rate**  
The percentage of emails delivered to the primary inbox versus spam or promotional folders.

**IP Address**  
The numerical address identifying the server sending your emails. Reputation is tied to IP addresses.

**IP Warming**  
Gradually increasing email volume on a new IP address to establish a positive sending reputation.

**Inbox Folder**  
The primary folder where recipients receive and view their emails, as opposed to spam, promotional, or other filtered folders.

## L

**Landing Page**  
A web page designed specifically for email campaign traffic, optimized for conversions.

**List Churn**  
The rate at which subscribers leave your list through unsubscribes, bounces, and complaints.

**List Fatigue**  
When subscribers become less responsive due to receiving too many emails or irrelevant content.

**List Hygiene**  
The practice of regularly cleaning your email list by removing inactive subscribers, bounces, and invalid addresses.

**List Segmentation**  
Dividing your email list into smaller groups based on demographics, behavior, preferences, or other criteria.

**LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados)**  
Brazil's General Data Protection Law, similar to GDPR, requiring explicit consent for email marketing and imposing significant penalties for violations.

## M

**MIME (Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions)**  
Standard for formatting non-text email attachments and multi-part messages including HTML and plain text.

**Mobile Optimization**  
Designing emails to display and function properly on mobile devices, crucial as 50%+ of emails are opened on mobile.

**Message Body**  
The main content area of an email, containing the text, images, and other elements that make up the message.

## N

**Newsletter (Email Newsletter/Electronic Bulletin)**  
A regularly distributed email publication containing news, updates, or content relevant to subscribers' interests. Also known as an electronic bulletin or email bulletin.

## O

**Open Rate**  
The percentage of delivered emails that were opened. Calculated as: (Unique opens ÷ Delivered emails) × 100.

**Opt-In**  
When someone gives permission to receive emails. Can be single opt-in or double opt-in.

**Opt-Out (Unsubscribe)**  
When someone requests to stop receiving emails. Must be honored immediately.

## P

**Permission-Based Email**  
Email sent only to recipients who have explicitly agreed to receive it. Required by law and best practice.

**Personalization**  
Customizing email content based on subscriber data, such as name, location, purchase history, or preferences.

**Phishing**  
Fraudulent emails attempting to steal personal information by impersonating legitimate organizations.

**Plain Text Email**  
Email without HTML formatting, containing only text. Often sent alongside HTML version as multi-part MIME.

**Preheader Text**  
The preview text appearing after the subject line in inbox views. Should complement the subject line.

**Privacy Policy**  
A legal document explaining how an organization collects, uses, and protects subscriber data. Required by privacy laws like GDPR and CASL.

## R

**Re-Engagement Campaign**  
Email series designed to win back inactive subscribers before removing them from your list.

**Recipient**  
The person who receives an email. Also called the email addressee or subscriber.

**Regional Data Protection Laws**  
Data protection regulations specific to different regions or countries, such as LGPD in Brazil, Argentina's PDPA, or Colombia's Law 1581, affecting email marketing practices.

**Render/Rendering**  
How an email displays in different email clients, browsers, and devices.

**Reply Rate**  
The percentage of recipients who replied to your email. High rates indicate strong engagement.

**Responsive Design**  
Email design that automatically adjusts layout and sizing for optimal viewing on any device.

**ROI (Return on Investment)**  
The revenue generated compared to email marketing costs. Email typically has the highest ROI of digital channels.

## S

**Seed List**  
Test email addresses used to monitor deliverability and rendering across different email clients and ISPs.

**Segmentation**  
Dividing your list into targeted groups for more relevant messaging. See List Segmentation.

**Sender Reputation**  
A score ISPs assign to sending IPs and domains based on engagement, complaints, and sending practices.

**Sender Score**  
A numerical reputation rating (0-100) for IP addresses, similar to a credit score for email senders.

**Sender Address**  
The email address from which messages are sent. Should be recognizable and consistent to build trust with recipients.

**Single Opt-In**  
Subscription process where users join a list with one action, without email confirmation.

**Soft Bounce**  
Temporary delivery failure due to full mailboxes or server issues. May succeed on retry.

**Spam (Unsolicited Bulk Email)**  
Unwanted email sent without permission. Damages sender reputation and violates laws.

**Spam Trap**  
Email addresses used to identify spammers. Includes recycled addresses and addresses that never opted in.

**SPF (Sender Policy Framework)**  
Authentication method specifying which IP addresses can send email for a domain.

**Subject Line**  
The email title visible in the inbox. Critical for open rates and must accurately represent content.

**Suppression List**  
A list of email addresses that should never receive emails, including unsubscribes, bounces, and complaints.

**Subscription Form**  
A web form used to collect email addresses and other subscriber information for building mailing lists.

## T

**Template**  
Pre-designed email layout that can be customized with content, ensuring consistent branding.

**Throttling**  
Controlling email sending speed to avoid overwhelming receiving servers or triggering spam filters.

**Transactional Email**  
Automated emails triggered by user actions (receipts, password resets, shipping notifications). Usually exempt from unsubscribe requirements.

**Triggered Email**  
Automated email sent based on specific subscriber behavior or data conditions.

## U

**Unique Click**  
Counting each recipient only once regardless of how many times they clicked links in an email.

**Unique Open**  
Counting each recipient only once regardless of how many times they opened an email.

**Unsubscribe Rate**  
The percentage of recipients who opted out. Calculated as: (Unsubscribes ÷ Delivered emails) × 100.

**Unsubscribe Message**  
The communication sent to users confirming their removal from an email list, often required by anti-spam laws.

## W

**Welcome Email**  
The first email sent to new subscribers, typically with the highest engagement rates.

**Web Analytics**  
The measurement and analysis of website data, often integrated with email marketing to track visitor behavior from email campaigns.

**Whitelist (Allowlist)**  
Approved sender list ensuring emails reach the inbox. Recipients can whitelist sender addresses.
