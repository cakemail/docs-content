# Adding a Video to a Campaign

## Description

You can use videos in your email campaign to provide interactive content and present your communications in a new and exciting way. While you cannot embed a video (i.e., your .mp4 file) directly into an email, you can include a link to it. This article details how to properly proceed.

## Why It's Important

Everyone loves watching videos. The recipient doesn't have to concentrate or spend time reading them, unlike reading an article, for example. In addition to having a head start over your competitors who send "classic" emails composed of text, images, and links, you can also make your emails stand out through the inclusion of a video. **In summary, an email that contains a video generates 3 times more clicks than one that contains an image.**

## How to Add a Video to a Campaign

Follow these steps to add a video to your campaign in the new email designer:

![](/images/campaign-creation/ajouter%20une%20video.gif)


<Stepper>

1. Upload your video to YouTube or Vimeo.

   The play icons can be customized in terms of shape and color.

</Stepper>

## Troubleshooting

**Video won't play**

- Remember that videos cannot play directly in emails
- Ensure the link directs to a functional video URL
- Test the video link first on the hosting platform

**Thumbnail image issues**

- Use high-quality images (recommended 1280x720 pixels)
- Ensure the image file size is optimized for email (under 200 KB)
- Add alt text for when images are blocked

**Low click rates**

- Make the play button overlay prominent and clickable
- Use engaging thumbnails that preview the video content
- Add compelling text encouraging viewers to click

**Mobile display issues**

- Test thumbnail sizes on mobile devices
- Ensure the play button is large enough for mobile touches
- Consider mobile-specific thumbnail dimensions

**Video platform restrictions**

- Check if the video is set to public or unlisted
- Verify that embedding is allowed on the hosting platform
- Consider using YouTube or Vimeo for better compatibility
