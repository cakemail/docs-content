# Email Deliverability

## Description

Everyone hates spam

Everyone hates spam. Moreover, inboxes are becoming increasingly sophisticated in determining whether something is important, unwanted, or spam.

How email filtering works: Some checks evaluate the content – or lack thereof – of an email for signs of spam, but the sender reputation from the email source usually plays a more important role in the analysis from an Internet Service Provider's perspective.

## Why it's important

Mastering deliverability basics ensures that your emails actually reach the inbox, not the spam folder — protecting your sender reputation and maximizing engagement. Without this, even the most brilliant message risks going completely unnoticed.

### Permission-based marketing:
- **Use a double opt-in system**: Only interested parties sign up to receive your email campaigns
- **Keep emails relevant**: Be clear about what you'll send when people sign up and stick to it

## Understanding the email address lifecycle

Keep in mind that valid email addresses can:

### Be automatically removed:
- **Role-based accounts**: If you try to add a role-based account, it will not be uploaded to your list
- **Spam traps**: Some addresses are automatically flagged and removed

### Deteriorate over time:
- **Closed accounts**: If the recipient closes their email account, these emails are "returned to sender" as permanently undeliverable
- **Address changes**: People change email addresses and old ones become invalid
- **Automatic removal**: The message should be removed from the contact list

## Benefits of professional email marketing

Professional email marketing services and tools:
- **Automatically remove undeliverable addresses**
- **Handle technical requirements** that can affect sender reputation
- **Monitor and maintain** your sender reputation
- **Manage compliance** with anti-spam regulations

## Timing your email sends

Getting emails delivered is obviously important, but knowing when to send them is too.

### Finding the right time:
- **No universal recommendation**: Timing varies by audience and industry
- **Key question**: When are your readers most likely to open and act on the email?
- **Best approach**: Combine understanding of your particular audience and email content with testing
- **Continuous optimization**: Find the answer that works best for your situation

## Key deliverability factors

1. **Sender reputation**: Most important factor in delivery decisions
2. **Permission-based lists**: Only send to people who have subscribed
3. **Content quality**: Relevant and valuable content reduces spam complaints
4. **List hygiene**: Regular cleaning of invalid addresses
5. **Authentication**: Proper domain and email authentication
6. **Engagement**: High open and click rates improve reputation
7. **Compliance**: Following anti-spam laws and regulations

## Best practices summary

- Use double opt-in for all subscriptions
- Send relevant content consistently
- Remove bouncing and invalid addresses
- Monitor your sender reputation
- Test sending times for your audience
- Use professional email marketing tools

## Troubleshooting

**My deliverability rates are declining**

- Analyze engagement metrics from the last 3-6 months for trends
- Check if you've changed content style, frequency, or list sources
- Review complaint rates and identify which campaigns triggered complaints
- Consider running a re-engagement campaign to clean your list

**Double opt-in is losing too many subscribers**

- Optimize the subject line and content of your confirmation email
- Send confirmation emails immediately (within minutes)
- Make the confirmation process as simple as possible (one click)
- Add instructions to check spam folder on your signup thank-you page

**I don't know when to send my emails**

- Start with industry benchmarks for your sector
- Run A/B tests by sending at different times and days
- Analyze your website traffic patterns for audience activity hours
- Consider time zones if you have a geographically diverse list

**My reputation seems damaged but I'm following best practices**

- Check if your domain or IP is on blacklists
- Review if team members have sent emails outside normal procedures
- Verify that your list hasn't been compromised or sold
- Consider using a dedicated IP if sharing is affecting your reputation
