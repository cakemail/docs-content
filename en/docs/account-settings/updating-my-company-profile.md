# Updating My Company Profile

## Description

Your company profile settings need to be up to date before sending your first campaign, as the law requires a physical address in every campaign.

## Why This Matters

- **Legal compliance**: Physical address is required in every campaign by law
- **Brand consistency**: Company logo can be used in your email campaigns
- **Professional appearance**: Complete profile information inspires trust
- **Contact information**: Provides recipients with legitimate business details

## Step-by-Step Instructions

To update your company profile, follow the instructions.

<Stepper>

1. Click on your company name/email address in the top right corner (this opens a dropdown menu), then click Account

1. Click on the **pencil button** to edit each section
</Stepper>

## Fields you can add/edit:

Here is a list of fields you can add/edit:

- **Company logo** (can be used in your emails)
- **Legal company name**
- **Address** 
- **Phone number**
- **Website**

## Best Practices

- **Keep information up to date**: Update details whenever your company information changes
- **Complete all fields**: Fill out all relevant fields for a professional appearance
- **High-quality logo**: Use a clear and professional logo image
- **Accurate address**: Ensure your physical address is correct and current
- **Valid contact information**: Provide working phone numbers and website URLs

## Troubleshooting

**Unable to upload company logo**

- Check file size (max 5 MB)
- Use PNG or JPG format
- Ensure image dimensions are reasonable
- Try reducing image resolution

**Address validation errors**
- Include all required fields
- Use standard address format
- Avoid special characters
- Check postal code format

**Changes are not saving**
- Click "Save" after each modification
- Check for validation errors
- Ensure all required fields are filled
- Try editing one field at a time

**Logo doesn't appear in emails**
- Publish changes after upload
- Check that email template includes logo merge tag
- Verify logo URL is accessible
- Clear email client cache

**Cannot edit company information**
- Confirm you have administrator privileges
- Check if account is suspended
- Ensure fields are not locked by organization
- Contact account owner for permissions

**Website URL not accepted**
- Include full URL (https://...)
- Remove trailing spaces
- Ensure URL is publicly accessible
- Use standard domain format
