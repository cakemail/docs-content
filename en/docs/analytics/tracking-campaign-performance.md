# Tracking Campaign Performance

## Description

Campaign performance tracking provides comprehensive analytics and reporting tools to measure the success of your email marketing efforts. This feature offers real-time insights into key metrics including open rates, click rates, bounce rates, and subscriber engagement, allowing you to monitor how recipients interact with your campaigns. The intuitive dashboard presents data in an easy-to-understand format, helping you make data-driven decisions to optimize your future campaigns.

In this article, we'll show you the tools at your disposal to track your email marketing success, or lack thereof, to better plan your campaigns in the future. Tracking email marketing results is crucial to your success.

## Why This Matters

Proper measurement of your email marketing performance is essential for better campaign planning. An effective campaign is characterized by a high open rate and low bounce rate, and one that receives few unsubscribes and SPAM complaints.

### The following terms are displayed for your review:

#### Open Rate
This is the percentage of people who opened the email.

You can increase open rates by being a recognized sender, writing a clear subject line, and using a good preheader.

#### Click Rate
This is the percentage of people who clicked on one of the links in your email.

Make your information easy to understand and give readers a clear call to action to increase your click rates.

#### Unsubscribe Rate
A subscriber may no longer wish to receive emails from you. In this case, if they click on the '[UNSUBSCRIBE]' link, they voluntarily remove themselves from your list.

#### Delivery Rate
The percentages of delivered and bounced emails are shown here.

#### Bounces
A bounce occurs when a campaign is not delivered to a recipient.

For more information on this topic, read our article.

#### Spam
Unsolicited email of a commercial nature. Currently we can only measure complaints for specific Internet Service Providers, but all Internet Service Providers track SPAM complaints, and the total number of complaints impacts your sender reputation.

**It should never exceed 0.25%**

## Troubleshooting

**Campaign statistics not updating**

- Allow 2-4 hours for real-time data to fully populate
- Check if campaign status shows "Sent" and not "Sending"
- Refresh your browser cache or try a different browser
- Verify your account has necessary permissions to view analytics

**Abnormally low open rates**

- Examine if your emails are landing in spam folders
- Check your sender reputation and authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
- Analyze if your subject lines might trigger spam filters
- Consider warming up your IP address if sending from a new domain

**Missing contact activity data**

- Ensure tracking pixels are enabled in your campaign settings
- Check if recipients have image blocking enabled in their email clients
- Verify that link tracking is enabled for the campaign
- Some corporate firewalls may block tracking - this is normal for B2B campaigns
