# Quebec French Translation - Lessons Learned

## Critical Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ NEVER Do This:

1. **Maple Leaf Emoji (🍁)**
   - This is Canadian, NOT Quebec-specific
   - Quebec has its own identity separate from pan-Canadian symbols
   - Use fleur-de-lis (⚜️) if you need a Quebec symbol

2. **"Tiguidou" Misuse**
   - Initial attempts used this incorrectly
   - It's dated and not commonly used in professional contexts
   - Avoid unless you're certain of proper usage

3. **Mechanical Transformations**
   - Example of what NOT to do: "de une gang de ta gang"
   - This creates broken, unnatural phrases
   - Always rewrite for natural flow

4. **Over-relying on Transformation Scripts**
   - Scripts are vocabulary guides only
   - Manual rewriting produces much better results
   - Natural phrasing > literal translation

## ✅ Best Practices

### Always Focus On:

1. **Tutoiement (tu/ton/tes)**
   - Use consistently throughout all content
   - Creates approachable, friendly tone
   - Standard in Quebec business communications

2. **Casual but Professional**
   - Not too formal (like France French)
   - Not too casual (still business content)
   - Find the sweet spot for Quebec market

3. **Modern, Authentic Expressions**
   - Use expressions actually used today
   - Avoid outdated Quebec French
   - When in doubt, use simpler language

4. **Technical Accuracy**
   - Never sacrifice clarity for local flavor
   - Technical terms should remain precise
   - Balance localization with accuracy

5. **Quebec-Specific References**
   - Reference Quebec (not Canada) when possible
   - Use Quebec personalities, places, events
   - Respect Quebec's distinct identity

## Examples of Good Adaptations

### Before (Standard French):
"Vous pouvez télécharger notre guide gratuit"

### After (Quebec French):
"Tu peux télécharger notre guide gratuit"

### Before (Mechanical):
"C'est une gang de fonctionnalités"

### After (Natural):
"C'est plein de fonctionnalités super utiles"
